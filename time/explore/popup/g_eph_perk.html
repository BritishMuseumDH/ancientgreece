Ephialtes and Perikles begin political reforms in Athens

Ephialtes and Perikles were two very important statesmen of Athens. Perikles would go on to be elected general, or strategos, many times over the next few decades.

The two statesmen reformed politics in Athens. They reduced the influence of the rich families of the city and gave more power to the public assembly where all male Athenian citizens could vote.
