Byzantine

After the Roman Empire had established its main capital in the east at Constantinople (Greek Byzantium) and lost its western provinces to the barbarians, Greece remained an important part of the eastern Christian Roman Empire. Ancient cities like Athens and Corinth were less important, but Thessalonica, in northern Greece, became the second city of the Empire. After 1204, much of Greece was occupied by western powers, such as the Franks and Venetians.
