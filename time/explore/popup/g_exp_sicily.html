Athenian expedition to Sicily

The Second Peloponnesian War ran between 431 and 404 BC.

By 415 BC, Athens was allied to two cities on the island of Sicily. It sailed to the island in the hope of gaining control of the whole of Sicily. This would be another source of grain for the city and would also help to weaken Sparta's position. However, the expedition ended in total failure. Almost all of Athens' fleet was destroyed and this left the city very vulnerable.
