The Crusades

The Crusades were a number of wars in which Western European Christians tried to seize control of Jerusalem from its Muslim rulers. They saw it as a 'holy war'. However the crusades often ended in bloody butchery.

During the Fourth Crusade, the crusaders started to capture Christian cities in the eastern Mediterranean, including the city of Constantinople. The crusaders divided the western part of the Byzantine Empire between them. Greece now came under the rule of the Franks, who came from the area of modern Germany.
