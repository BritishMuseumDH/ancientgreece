Cycladic culture on Cycladic islands, 3200 - 2000 BC

The Cycladic culture flourished in the Cyclades, the group of islands in the middle of the Aegean Sea, between about 3200 - 2000 BC. The islanders lived by farming and were also expert ship-builders and sailors. Amongst their best-known products are female figurines with folded arms, made from local marble.
