title=&page1=Tonight is a very special occasion. We are performing a sacrifice to Athena. Everyone has gathered together to join in the procession. We've all washed and dressed and put on our best robes. I hold a garland and others hold ribbons.&page1b=A sacrifice was one of the most important ways to honour the gods. A sacrifice meant that an animal would be killed and offered up to a god. The event would usually begin with a procession. People would dress in their best clothes. The two people shown here hold garlands, these are wreaths made of twigs or small branches.&page2=Goats and cows are led to the altar. They are very fine animals. I spent all day tying ribbons onto our goat, and painting his horns. Our goat was not too happy about all the attention. He kicked and fidgeted all day!&page2b=The animals that were to be sacrificed had to be very healthy. A sickly animal would have been an insult to the gods. Domestic animals, like sheep, cows, goats, hens and oxen, were the most common animal to be used at these ceremonies.

Sacrificial animals were often decorated with ribbons and their horns were painted with gold.
